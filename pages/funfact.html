More Than Just a Side Project

While many know Daniel Kim from Wave to Earth, The Poles was actually his first musical home, formed back in 2017. If Wave to Earth is a warm sunset, The Poles is the cool night breeze. They focus on the raw, unpolished energy of British Rock combined with the dreamy atmosphere of Shoegaze.

A Spectrum of Emotions

The Poles is a South Korean indie rock band that has carved out a unique space in the music scene with their atmospheric and emotionally resonant sound. Formed by Daniel Kim (the visionary frontman also known for his work in the band Wave to Earth), the group focuses on a raw, guitar-driven aesthetic that leans heavily into Britpop and alternative rock influences. Since their debut, they have become known for creating music that feels both nostalgic and refreshingly modern, capturing the bittersweet complexities of youth and longing.


The band’s discography is characterized by its "gray-scale" emotional palette—music that feels like a rainy afternoon or a quiet moment of reflection. Their breakthrough tracks, such as those found on their EPs From the Outset and The High Tide, showcase a sophisticated blend of melodic hooks and shoegaze-inspired textures. While Daniel Kim’s soulful vocals often take center stage, the tight instrumentation of the band provides a powerful backdrop that elevates their sound from simple indie-pop to cinematic rock.


Despite the mainstream dominance of K-Pop, The Poles have managed to cultivate a dedicated global following by staying true to their underground roots. They represent a growing movement of Korean artists who prioritize creative autonomy and genre-bending experimentation. As they continue to release new material and perform at major festivals, The Poles remain a vital voice for listeners seeking authenticity, depth, and a sonic escape into the more melancholic side of the human experience.
